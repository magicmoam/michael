FEBRUARY 10, 2026

⚖️ Jury Service: E.I.F.

Everything Is Fucked — a day of justice on the Rock

📬 The Kitchen — 7:12 AM

El Vrada is standing in the kitchen in his dressing gown, holding an official-looking letter. His face suggests he has just been personally insulted by the Crown.
EL VRADA:
No. No no no. NO.
SARAH:
What is it?
EL VRADA:
Jury service. They want me for JURY SERVICE.
SARAH:
That's your civic duty.
EL VRADA:
My civic duty is paying council tax and complaining about potholes. Not sitting in a room with eleven strangers deciding whether Terry from Catalan Bay nicked a generator.
SARAH:
You don't know what the case is yet.
EL VRADA:
It's always a generator. Or a moped. Or someone's cousin's mate from the estate who "didn't know the bag was in the boot."
📝 El Vrada once served as a witness in a parking dispute and still brings it up at dinner parties. He considers this his legal career.

🏛️ The Supreme Court — 8:55 AM

El Vrada arrives at the Supreme Court building on Main Street. It is not, by any international standard, supreme. He is wearing his "court suit" which is just his wedding suit with a different tie. The tie has tiny anchors on it.
COURT CLERK:
Name?
EL VRADA:
El Vrada. Here under protest.
COURT CLERK:
Take a seat in the waiting room, please.
EL VRADA:
How long will this take?
COURT CLERK:
Could be all day.
EL VRADA:
(under his breath) Everything is fucked.

🪑 The Waiting Room — 9:00 AM to ???

The waiting room is beige. Aggressively beige. There are twelve plastic chairs, a water cooler with no cups, and a poster about fire safety from 2004. El Vrada is seated between a woman doing a crossword and a man who appears to be asleep.
EL VRADA:
(to the crossword woman) How long have you been here?
CROSSWORD WOMAN:
Forty-five minutes.
EL VRADA:
And nothing's happened?
CROSSWORD WOMAN:
The clerk came in and said "it won't be long."
EL VRADA:
When?
CROSSWORD WOMAN:
Forty-five minutes ago.
El Vrada stares at the fire safety poster. It says "In case of fire, leave the building." He considers this.
EL VRADA:
(muttering) There must be a lighter in this building somewhere...

🥪 The Sad Sandwich — 12:30 PM

It is lunchtime. El Vrada has been given a lunch voucher worth £3.50. He is at the café across the road. The only sandwich left is egg and cress.
EL VRADA:
(on the phone to Sarah) They gave me three pound fifty for lunch. Do you know what three pound fifty buys in Gibraltar? Half an egg sandwich and a bottle of water that might actually just be tap.
SARAH:
You could've brought your own lunch.
EL VRADA:
I'm not bringing a PACKED LUNCH to the Supreme Court, Sarah. I'm a juror, not a Year 6 on a school trip.
SARAH:
Have they even called you in yet?
EL VRADA:
NO. We've been sitting there for three and a half hours. The sleeping man woke up, ate a Twix, and went back to sleep. The crossword woman finished two puzzles. I've read the fire safety poster nine times. I now know all emergency exits. I AM the fire marshal.
📝 The egg sandwich was, by all accounts, damp. El Vrada ate it with the expression of a man who has lost a war.

🐒 The Monkey Incident — 2:15 PM

Back in the waiting room. A Barbary macaque has somehow gotten into the corridor outside. The clerk is trying to shoo it away with a clipboard.
EL VRADA:
(standing up, energised) RIGHT. Finally, some action.
CROSSWORD WOMAN:
Is that a monkey?
EL VRADA:
That's a Barbary macaque and technically it's got more right to be here than I do.
The monkey grabs the sleeping man's Twix wrapper and runs off. The sleeping man does not wake up.
EL VRADA:
(to the clerk) Is THIS the trial? Because honestly I'd rather judge the monkey.

🔨 The Anti-Climax — 4:00 PM

The clerk returns. Everyone looks up with desperate hope.
COURT CLERK:
Thank you all for your patience. The case has been settled out of court. You're free to go.
Silence. El Vrada stands up slowly.
EL VRADA:
I'm sorry — what?
COURT CLERK:
The case has been resolved. You won't be needed.
EL VRADA:
I've been here for EIGHT HOURS. I ate a sandwich that tasted like regret. A monkey stole a Twix. I memorised the fire escape plan. And you're telling me it's just... OVER?
COURT CLERK:
You'll receive your compensation by post.
EL VRADA:
Compensation? How much?
COURT CLERK:
£10.22 for the day.
EL VRADA:
(voice cracking) ...Ten pounds twenty-two?
COURT CLERK:
Minus the lunch voucher.
EL VRADA:
So... six pound seventy-two. For eight hours. That's... (calculating) ...eighty-four pence an hour. I am being paid EIGHTY-FOUR PENCE AN HOUR to do NOTHING.
📝 For reference, the Barbary macaques are estimated to bring in roughly £5 million a year in tourism revenue. The monkey earned more per minute stealing a Twix than El Vrada did all day.

🏠 Home — That Evening

El Vrada is back on the sofa. He has the look of a man who has seen things. Sarah hands him a cup of tea.
SARAH:
So how was justice?
EL VRADA:
There was no justice, Sarah. Only chairs. And egg. And a monkey. And a man who slept through the entire British legal system.
SARAH:
Will you do it again if they call you?
EL VRADA:
(long pause) ...They might have better sandwiches next time.
SARAH:
That's the spirit.
EL VRADA:
Don't push it.
RECEIPT OF JUSTICE
Hours served: 8
Cases judged: 0
Sandwiches endured: 1
Monkeys encountered: 1
Fire exits memorised: ALL
Twix stolen: 1
Compensation: £10.22
Less lunch: -£3.50
Less parking: -£6.00
NET PROFIT: £0.72
⚖️ E.I.F. ⚖️
Everything Is Fucked.
